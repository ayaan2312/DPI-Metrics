Understanding Mouse Performance

Every click, every swipe — your mouse is constantly talking to your computer. Here's what it's actually saying, and why the numbers matter.

PRECISION TOOL
Hz

Polling Rate

How often your mouse speaks to your PC, per second

Your mouse sends its position to your computer at a fixed rate measured in Hertz (Hz). At 125 Hz, that's every 8 milliseconds. At 1000 Hz, every 1 millisecond. The difference might sound small — but in a fast-paced game, those 7 extra milliseconds are the gap between hitting your shot and missing it.

DPI

Dots Per Inch

How far your cursor travels for every inch of physical movement

DPI tells your operating system how many pixels to move the cursor for each inch your mouse physically moves. A 400 DPI mouse moves 400 pixels per inch. A 1600 DPI mouse moves 1600. Higher isn't always better — professional players often use 400–800 DPI and a higher in-game multiplier to reduce sensor noise.

Sensor Jitter

The invisible noise your sensor adds to every movement

Even when you try to move your mouse in a perfect straight line, the sensor reports tiny random deviations. This is jitter — caused by the sensor's optical noise floor, surface texture variations, and firmware processing. The Jitter Plot tab lets you see your sensor's actual noise signature.

How This Tool Works

Browser-based measurement using the PointerEvent API

DPI Metrics uses getCoalescedEvents() on Chromium browsers to access intermediate mouse samples between display frames — getting closer to the true hardware polling rate than standard mousemove events allow. All computation runs in requestAnimationFrame loops with pre-allocated typed arrays — zero garbage collection during measurement.

⚠ Browser Limitation

Even with getCoalescedEvents(), Chromium timestamps coalesced events at the display frame boundary — not actual hardware interrupt time. A 1000 Hz mouse on a 240 Hz monitor will measure ~240 Hz max. For true hardware verification, a native OS tool using Windows Raw Input is needed.

100% Local Processing

All mouse data is processed in your browser's memory. No coordinates, timings, or measurements are transmitted anywhere. Zero tracking. Zero telemetry.
